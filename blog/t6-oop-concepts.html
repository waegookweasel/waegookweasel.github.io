Object-Oriented Programming

All the Things Are Objects?

As a new programmer with only ruby experience, it's difficult to make a case for why object oriented programming is superior to procedural languages. The book Practical Object-Oriented Design in Ruby has been helpful in developing my understanding of the advantages of object-oriented programming (OOP).

It seems that OOP was developed to better manage communication between objects. Because of this communications management, changing or adjusting a piece of code is far less likely to destroy the project. Change is inevitable and even the best made applications will likely need to be adjusted or changed.

Procedural languages seem to be a less desirable alternative to object-oriented languages. Procedural languages wrap data into variables and pass them to behaviors. From what I gather, this complicates accessibility and obfuscates what’s happening to and with the data.

Object-oriented languages do not guarantee ease of change. It’s important to use object-oriented design principles and to consider the flexibility to make future changes while programming.
